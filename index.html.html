Key Finding
Free steak treatment causally increased 5W AOR in 7 of 13 cohorts. Positive direction observed in 10/13 weeks. No evidence of harm detected in any cohort.
Effect is meaningful but not universal
Effect varies by week, customer segment, and likely menu context. The treatment does not produce a uniform effect across all conditions. Range: +6.5% to +15.1% across significant cohorts.
Targeting Signal Detected
Loyalty, box size, and surcharge history are the strongest moderators (loyalty dominates in 12/13 weeks). However, the direction of these effects has not yet been established — stratified analysis is required before any targeting implementation.
Overall Assessment
The evidence supports a causal positive effect of the steak investment on ordering behavior. The effect is not universal — it varies by week, customer segment, and likely by menu context. The behavioral evidence is strong. The financial and operational case requires further analysis before permanent integration decisions. Immediate analytical priorities: loyalty stratification and extended measurement window, completable within 2 weeks using existing data.

Phase Weeks Result Context
1. Initial W40–W41 2/2 Sig. (+9–10%) Both weeks significant. One possible explanation: novelty of free steak format.
2. Attenuation W42–W46 1/5 Significant W43 significant (+12.1%). W42 & W46 are genuine high-power nulls (N = 3,500 and 3,451).
3. Resurgence W47–W50 4/4 Sig. (+6.5–15%) Measurement windows span holiday season. Strongest effects. Seasonal interaction vs. confound not yet separated.
4. Holiday W51–W52 0/2 Significant Deep holiday measurement windows. W52 possibly underpowered (N = 1,227).
W48 — Inverted Pool Ratio: Strongest result (+15.1%, p<0.001) but only week where N Control (2,670) < N Treatment (5,101). Inverted ratio warrants investigation into whether cap sell-out timing introduced selection dynamics not captured by matching.
W51–W52 — Design Contamination: Steak recipe 1017 did not sell out in these weeks, undermining the control group definition. These cohorts should be interpreted with significant caution.

Q4 — What's Next?
Analytical Next Steps
Recommended Analytical Follow-Ups
1. Customer Feature Driver Deep Dive
a.
Loyalty-stratified ATE: Compute average treatment effect within loyalty quartiles across all 13 cohorts. Determine whether high-tenure, low-tenure, or mid-tenure customers show the largest treatment effect.
b.
Box size + surcharge stratified ATE: Same approach for the #2 and #3 CATE moderators. Determine direction of effect for each group.
2. Menu Composition Overlay
Cross-reference weekly menu composition and specific steak recipes with treatment effect magnitudes across all 13 weeks. Objective: identify whether specific menu contexts amplify or attenuate the steak effect. This addresses the W42/W46 genuine null results.
